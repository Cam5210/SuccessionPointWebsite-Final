2025 M&A Outlook: Record Activity Expected as 35% of Advisors Near Retirement

The wealth management M&A landscape is poised for unprecedented activity in 2025, with over 40% of RIA leaders expecting increased transaction volume—a substantial jump from just 18% in 2023. This surge is driven by a perfect storm of factors: an aging advisor workforce, favorable valuations, and evolving deal structures.

Key findings from our latest market analysis reveal that average EBITDA multiples have stabilized at 9.2x, while recurring revenue multiples have increased to 3.08x. Perhaps most significantly, only 1% of advisors have a written succession plan shared with relevant parties, despite 43% planning to retire within the next decade.

For sellers, this creates an opportune environment with strong buyer demand and competitive valuations. For buyers, the abundance of acquisition targets presents strategic growth opportunities, though selectivity remains crucial. The evolution toward longer-term partnerships—with transition periods extending from 7-12 years—reflects a fundamental shift in how the industry approaches succession.

"We're witnessing the largest transfer of wealth management practices in history. Advisors who act strategically now will capture maximum value, while those who wait may find themselves with fewer options."
