    Frequently Asked Questions

    Common questions about practice transitions and M&A services

    What makes Succession Point different from other M&A firms?

    With 35+ years focused solely on wealth management transitions, deep industry connections, and unmatched expertise, we deliver the best matches and results.

    How long does the typical M&A process take?

    Timeline varies based on your specific situation and objectives. A straightforward practice sale typically takes 3-9 months from initial consultation to closing. Strategic partnerships or complex monetization structures may take 6-12 months. We work at your pace to ensure optimal outcomes.

    What revenue multiples can I expect for my practice?

    Multiples range from 3-16x top-line revenue depending on practice characteristics, deal structure, and buyer type. Full acquisitions typically achieve 3-8x top line revenue, while strategic partnerships can reach 3-16x. Factors include AUM, revenue quality, client demographics, and growth trajectory.

    Do I have to retire immediately after selling my practice?

    No. Many advisors continue working post-transaction. Options include staying on as an employee, consultant, or partner. Strategic partnerships allow you to monetize while maintaining control and continuing to serve clients. We customize structures to fit your timeline and goals.

    How does Succession Point get paid?

    Our consultation services are provided at NO COST to advisors. We're compensated by the acquiring firms or strategic partners upon successful transaction completion. This aligns our interests with yours - we only succeed when you achieve your objectives.

    Will my clients be taken care of after the transition?

    Absolutely. We carefully vet all buyers and partners to ensure they share your commitment to client service. Our matching process considers investment philosophy, service standards, and cultural fit to enable seamless transitions and continued excellent care for your clients.
